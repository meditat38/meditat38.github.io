hopohopo

A VR-Experience about social anxiety on the Oculus Quest

The virtual reality experience "Hopohopo" invites you to accompany a person affected by social anxiety in their everyday life. The interactive story conveys an impression of the challenges faced by people suffering from social phobia. The application is intended to educate and raise awareness about this mental illness.


In the story, viewers accompany Hope, a person who experiences severe social anxiety, in parts of her everyday life. They experience social situations that are very challenging for Hope, such as a ride in a bus full of people or a lecture in front of an audience. The VR application uses strong visual, auditory, and haptic stimuli to engage in the event.


Through this immersive approach, the VR application "Hopohopo" provides basic knowledge about the clinical picture of social phobia. For affected people, it can be a great help if, for example, close people are well informed about the accompanying symptoms of their disorder and can provide appropriate support.

Julia Drost and I developed the concept for the VR experience in the course "Rethinking Tech Resources for Mental Health. XR-Prototyping etc." at the FH Potsdam by Rahel Flechtner, Sebastian Wloch and Prof. Dr. Frank Heidmann. After finishing the course Julia Drost and I got the offer to further develop the application for the research project of the FH Potsdam "DISA - Digital Inclusion in the Context of Social Anxiety Disorder" (DISA) and to design the story together with people affected by social phobia.


Julia Drost took more care of the story and the creation of the voice recordings and I was responsible for the technical and visual implementation of the VR experience.

The VR experience was exhibited at the Mensch und Computer 2022 conference at Darmstadt University of Technology from September 4-7, 2022, and was nominated for the DIVR Science Award on September 27, 2022, in the Athletic Performance, Psychology & Health category.


"Hopohopo" is freely available in English and German versions on Meta AppLab and SideQuest.
